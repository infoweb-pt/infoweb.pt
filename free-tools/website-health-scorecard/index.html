      Frequently Asked Questions

      What is a website health score? ▾
      A website health score is a 0–100 rating that measures how well your site performs across five critical areas: page speed, mobile-friendliness, SSL security, meta tag completeness, and Google Business presence. A higher score means your site is more likely to rank well in search and convert visitors into customers.
      How do I check if my website is mobile-friendly? ▾
      Our tool simulates mobile viewport detection and checks for responsive design patterns. For a real-world test, open your site on a smartphone or use Chrome DevTools' device emulation. If text is too small, buttons are hard to tap, or horizontal scrolling is needed, your site needs responsive improvements.
      Why does my website need an SSL certificate? ▾
      An SSL certificate (HTTPS) encrypts data between your visitors and your server. Without it, browsers show "Not Secure" warnings, which scare visitors away. Google also uses HTTPS as a ranking signal, so lacking it hurts your search visibility. Free certificates are available from Let's Encrypt.
      What meta tags does my website need for SEO? ▾
      Every page needs a unique <title> tag (under 60 characters), a meta description (150–160 characters), and a viewport meta tag for mobile. These help search engines understand your content and influence how your pages appear in search results. Missing or duplicate tags hurt your rankings.
