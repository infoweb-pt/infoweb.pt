# How to Optimize Your Google Business Profile for More Local Leads

For many local businesses, Google Business Profile (GBP) is the front door — not the website. People see the map pack, check hours, read reviews, and tap **Call** or **Directions** before they ever visit your site.

Optimizing GBP is free and high leverage. This guide walks through the settings that actually move calls, bookings, and direction requests.

## Claim and verify first

If you have not claimed the profile, do that before anything else. Verify by postcard, phone, or email as offered. Unclaimed listings let competitors or strangers suggest edits.

Keep the business name exact — no keyword stuffing like “Best Plumber Manchester | Cheap”.

## Categories and services

### Primary category

Pick the one category that best matches what you **sell**, not what you hope to rank for. A dental clinic should not pick “Marketing agency” because it sounds broader.

### Secondary categories

Add legitimate secondary categories only when you truly offer those services.

### Services list

Add each service with a short description and price range where appropriate. This feeds relevance for “near me” searches.

## Photos and visuals

Upload real photos on a schedule:

- Storefront or van branding
- Team at work
- Before/after project shots
- Menu items or treatment rooms (industry dependent)

Profiles with fresh photos get more engagement. Avoid stock-only galleries.

## Reviews (the trust engine)

- Ask happy customers right after a job — timing matters
- Reply to every review, especially negative ones (professional, factual)
- Never buy fake reviews — Google removes them and may penalize the profile

Use a direct review link so customers do not search for the button. Our [review link generator](https://infoweb.sousadev.com/free-tools/google-review-generator/) builds that URL.

## Posts, offers, and updates

GBP posts are micro-updates visible on the profile:

- Seasonal offers
- New service launches
- Holiday hours
- Event participation

Post at least twice a month so the profile looks alive.

## Q&A section

Seed common questions yourself (as a customer would):

- “Do you offer emergency call-outs?”
- “Is parking available?”
- “Do you serve [area]?”

Monitor Q&A — anyone can ask; answer quickly to prevent wrong public answers.

## Website and action links

Link to the **best conversion page**, not a generic homepage if a booking page exists.

Add action buttons Google supports for your category:

- Book appointment
- Order online
- Get quote

When visitors click through, the website must load fast on mobile and repeat the same promise they saw on GBP.

## NAP consistency

Name, Address, Phone must match your website and citations. A typo in the postcode or old mobile number splits trust signals.

## Measure what matters

In GBP insights, track monthly:

- Calls from profile
- Direction requests
- Website clicks
- Search queries shown (where available)

If website clicks are high but leads are low, fix the landing page — GBP did its job.

## Common mistakes

- Leaving “open hours” on default holiday settings
- Ignoring message requests in the GBP inbox
- Website link pointing to Facebook instead of owned site
- No photos for 12 months
- Keyword-stuffed business description

## Tie GBP to your website strategy

GBP wins discovery. The website wins depth: service pages, FAQs, pricing context, forms, and email capture. Together they beat either channel alone.

## Bottom line

Treat Google Business Profile as a product, not a one-time setup. Accurate categories, real photos, steady reviews, fresh posts, and clean website links turn map visibility into calls — then let your site finish the conversion.
