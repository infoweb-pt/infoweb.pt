# Domain Name Checklist Before You Buy One

Buying a domain takes five minutes. Fixing a bad domain choice can take years. Use this tactical checklist before you pay — it complements our longer [domain guide](https://infoweb.sousadev.com/blog/posts/choosing-perfect-domain/) with pre-purchase checks only.

## Quick checklist

- [ ] Easy to say out loud on a phone call
- [ ] Short enough to type from a van sign or receipt
- [ ] No confusing spelling (0 vs O, 1 vs l)
- [ ] Trademark / existing brand search done
- [ ] Right extension for your market (.com for international, .co.uk for UK local, etc.)
- [ ] Matching social handles available or acceptable alternative
- [ ] Renewal price checked (year 2+ not just promo year 1)
- [ ] Registered to an email you control (not only the agency’s)
- [ ] DNS access documented
- [ ] Privacy/WHOIS settings understood

## 1. Say it, spell it, share it

Ask three people to repeat the domain after hearing it once. If they spell it wrong, customers will too.

**Good:** `oakplumbing.com`

**Risky:** `xplumb-247-uk.com`

## 2. Length and readability

Aim for roughly 6–14 characters before the extension. Avoid hyphens unless you have a strong reason — they are easy to forget when typing.

## 3. Trademark and reputation check

Search:

- Google for the exact name + your industry
- UK/EU trademark databases if you operate there
- Social platforms for impersonation or bad reviews on a similar name

A cheap domain is not cheap if you receive a cease-and-desist letter.

## 4. Choose the extension on purpose

| Extension | When it fits |
|-----------|----------------|
| `.com` | Default for most international businesses |
| `.co.uk` | UK-local trust signal |
| `.org` | Associations; sometimes used by clinics/education |
| Industry TLDs | Only if customers already expect them |

Do not buy five extensions on day one unless you will use them. Park the main one, add others when revenue justifies it.

## 5. Social handle alignment

Check Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn, and TikTok handles. Exact match helps brand recall. If taken, decide a consistent alternative before printing materials.

## 6. True cost = renewal, not promo

Registrars advertise first-year discounts. Check:

- Year 2 renewal price
- Privacy protection fees
- Transfer-out fees (should be reasonable)

A $1 first year with a $40 renewal is a pricing trick, not a bargain.

## 7. Ownership and access

Register with an email you control (`you@yourbusiness.com` or a secure owner inbox). Store:

- Registrar login
- 2FA backup codes
- DNS panel access

If an agency buys the domain, contract must state you own it and can transfer it.

## 8. DNS control

You need to point the domain to hosting, email, and verification records (Google, Microsoft, etc.). Losing DNS access means losing the ability to move the site.

## After you buy

1. Enable auto-renew with a valid card
2. Turn on 2FA on the registrar account
3. Connect the domain to your website and SSL
4. Set up professional email when ready (`hello@yourbusiness.com`)

## Bottom line

The best domain is easy to say, easy to type, legally safe, affordable to renew, and owned by you. Run this checklist once and you avoid the expensive rename later.
